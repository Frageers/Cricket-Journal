The blackjack experiment

This is the webpage summarizing the blackjack experiment I did on the crickets

Rules:

Before we begin, I will set out some ground rules to try and reduce the outside factors that could help the crickets learn blackjack faster or make them learn it slower

The first rule is that no outside blackjack influence is allowed on the crickets

What this means is that only I will be able to do anything related to teaching the crickets blackjack

This means no youtube video, not my brother or my parents or a friend, only me

We will also be excluding doubling and splitting as they can be hard to grasp

Summary

The first few days were all belonging to teaching the crickets the cards and their values

When allocating the time slots for this experiment I only put down 5 days to teach them about the cards

What ended up happening was it took 2.5 weeks so I had no time to get to the rules

Conclusion

Due to the fact that I could not teach the crickets the rules, but I could teach them some of the cards, I will take it as a 10% success

What I could have changed was making it easier for the crickets to tell me the value of the card

What I did was show the card and they could go near the piece of paper which had the value of the card

This could have lead to me understanding the crickets wrong

What I would do different

If I could do things again what I would do differently is that I would make it so the crickets could tell me the value of the cards easier and with more accuracy with my understanding of what they were trying to say

The first thought I had was like those buttons that help dogs speak but that be too hard for the cricket to press down

Maybe I could also use normal playing cards because I used my golden playing cards which could have been a distraction for the crickets

Another thing to change was maybe the incentive for the crickets

I could have tried to identify what the crickets liked and used that as a prize instead of just adding more carrots after every successful session
